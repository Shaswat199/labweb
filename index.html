Who We Are

Decoding Bone & Immune System Interactions

The OsteoImmunology and Bone Metabolism Laboratory at IIT Bhilai is dedicated to understanding the molecular dialogue between the immune system and skeletal tissue. Our research spans from fundamental cellular biology to translational science, aiming to develop novel therapeutic strategies for bone diseases.

Led by Dr. Ashish Kumar Tripathi, our lab integrates molecular biology, cellular metabolism, and pharmacology. We study how conditions such as HIV, sickle cell disease, obesity, and metabolic disorders disrupt bone homeostasis — and how we can intervene to restore it.

We collaborate with leading institutions worldwide including Emory University, and our work has been published in high-impact journals including Cell Death & Disease, Journal of Infectious Diseases, ACS Pharmacology & Translational Science, and Free Radical Biology & Medicine.

What We Study

Research Areas

Four interconnected research tracks addressing critical gaps in bone and immune system biology.

01
🦴
OsteoImmunology
Investigating molecular crosstalk between immune cells and bone-forming/resorbing cells. We study how immune dysregulation in HIV, aging, and inflammation drives pathological bone loss and how cytokines like IL-4 and immune reconstitution alter skeletal homeostasis.
02
⚡
Mitochondrial Bioenergetics
Exploring how mitochondrial energy metabolism governs osteoblast and osteoclast differentiation. Our work on hydroxyurea and sickle cell disease revealed how disrupted mitochondrial function exacerbates trabecular bone loss, opening new therapeutic avenues.
03
🔗
Iron Overload & Ferroptosis
Studying the role of iron dysregulation and ferroptotic cell death in skeletal pathology. We investigate how excess iron accumulation in sickle cell disease and metabolic disorders triggers oxidative stress and drives bone cell dysfunction.
04
🧬
Bone Remodeling in Metabolic Disorders
Examining how obesity, glucocorticoid excess, and lipotoxicity impair bone marrow mesenchymal stem cell fate, shift the balance toward adipogenesis, and compromise skeletal integrity. We identify small molecule and natural compound interventions.
